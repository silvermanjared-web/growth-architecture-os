# Thought Leadership

This section collects public-facing points of view from articles, platform shifts, media strategy themes, and source-backed commentary.

The purpose is to show how Jared thinks about changes in attention, trust, measurement, and channel behavior beyond any single campaign or role.

## Files

- `creator-economy-election-media.md` — creator and influencer strategy in election media
- `ott-video-first-social.md` — linear TV, OTT, video-first social, and modern video planning
- `media-behavior-notes.md` — concise notes on how attention and trust move across platforms

## Core themes

- Attention fragments before budgets do.
- Trust often moves closer to communities and creators.
- Legacy channels can still work when planned with modern data and coordinated messaging.
- New channels should be tested with discipline, not hype.
- Measurement maturity should influence how quickly the media mix moves.

## How to use this section

Use this section for:

- interview discussion
- public POV development
- CMO or founder conversations
- LinkedIn and website positioning
- examples of media strategy judgment

## Standard

Do not chase every platform trend. Understand the shift, define the channel role, measure what matters, and build the operating model to manage it.
