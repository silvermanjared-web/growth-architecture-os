# Thought Leadership: Creator Economy and Election Media

## Source

Digiday: `As the presidential election approaches, where do influencers come in?`

## Why it matters

This article gives third-party press validation for Jared's point of view on creator-led political media, younger audiences, and the shift from traditional campaign communication to social-native influence.

## Jared's quoted POV

The article cites Jared Silverman, then senior director of paid media at NP Digital, on the increasing impact of influencers in elections.

The core idea: creator relationships can function like a modern version of community-level campaigning because creators already have trust, distribution, and algorithmic relevance with their audiences.

## Strategic interpretation

This is not just an influencer-marketing quote. It fits a larger operating thesis:

- Distribution has fragmented.
- Trust has moved closer to creators and communities.
- Younger audiences discover information through feeds, not just institutions.
- Paid, earned, organic, and creator media increasingly overlap.
- Campaigns need channel strategy that reflects how attention actually moves.
