# Thought Leadership: OTT, Linear TV, and Video-First Social

## Source

LinkedIn article by Jared Silverman, published December 15, 2022: `Is It Time for Advertisers to Leave Linear TV for OTT and Video-First Social?`

## Core thesis

Advertisers should not abandon linear TV just because OTT and video-first social are growing. The smarter move is to modernize the video mix: use better data, coordinate linear and digital messaging, test emerging channels, and watch measurement maturity closely.

## What the article argues

The article came out of a conference on the TV and video landscape. The surface-level theme was familiar: reduce reliance on linear TV, increase OTT, and invest more in video-first social platforms such as TikTok.

Jared's read was more nuanced.

Linear TV still matters because it can produce volume and impact that digital-first plans may struggle to match. At the same time, linear cannot be planned in a broad, outdated way. It needs to be optimized with modern audience data, coordinated with digital touchpoints, and evaluated against real business outcomes.

## Case study inside the article

Jared shared a TBC case study for a homecare client seeking new employees.

The team knew Facebook was working, but also believed the audience was watching linear TV. The question was whether the media buy was reaching the right programming and whether the same audience could be reached more effectively elsewhere.

Using TVSquared analytics, the team found counterintuitive audience behavior, including unexpected programming patterns among women over 45. That insight helped reshape the buy.

The team then treated linear TV and Facebook as coordinated narrative layers:

- Linear TV introduced the value of the brand.
- Facebook carried more specific benefit messaging.
- The combined plan captured more audience without requiring a larger budget.

## Larger strategic point

The article is not anti-linear and not blindly pro-OTT.

The real point is that video strategy should follow attention, data quality, measurement maturity, and audience behavior.

Linear TV, Meta, and Google remained highly relevant in the article's framing, especially when paired with stronger analytics and coordinated messaging. OTT and video-first social were positioned as urgent testing grounds, with broader rollout likely as measurement and standards mature.

## Why this belongs in the repo

This article shows Jared's platform-shift thinking before it became consensus language.

It demonstrates:

- Cross-channel media judgment
- Comfort with nuance instead of absolutist platform takes
- Understanding of second-screen behavior
- Practical use of analytics to improve traditional media
- Early attention to OTT and video-first social measurement maturity
- A bias toward test-and-learn rather than trend chasing

## Reusable positioning line

Jared's video strategy POV is pragmatic: do not abandon what still works, but do not confuse current effectiveness with future security. Test emerging video channels now, modernize the legacy buy, and let better measurement determine how quickly the mix should move.
