# AI Governance and Risk

## Principle

AI should increase leverage without lowering judgment.

The right question is not `Can AI do this?` The right question is `Can AI help us do this more clearly, safely, and repeatably?`

## Risk categories

### Confidentiality

Do not paste sensitive company, employee, legal, financial, or customer information into tools without approval.

### Accuracy

AI can sound confident while being wrong. Claims, numbers, and source references need review.

### Tone

AI can over-polish, overstate, or flatten nuance. Executive communication still requires human judgment.

### Ownership

AI should support decisions, not obscure who is accountable for them.

### Adoption

A workflow that people do not use is not a workflow. It is a demo.

## Review checklist

Before using AI-assisted output, check:

- Is the claim supported?
- Is any confidential detail included unnecessarily?
- Does the tone fit the audience?
- Is the recommendation clear?
- Are limitations or caveats visible?
- Is human ownership clear?

## Good AI use

- Summarizing known information
- Drafting first-pass memos
- Checking tone and structure
- Turning repeated work into templates
- Surfacing possible risks
- Creating reusable playbooks

## Poor AI use

- Inventing support for a claim
- Replacing judgment in sensitive decisions
- Producing polished but vague communication
- Automating broken processes
- Creating workflows without adoption planning

## Standard

AI should make the work more useful, not just faster.
