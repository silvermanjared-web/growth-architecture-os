# AI Operating Model

## Principle

AI only matters when it changes how work gets done.

The goal is not novelty. The goal is useful leverage: faster diagnosis, cleaner drafts, stronger QA, better reporting, and less repetitive manual work.

## Where AI is useful

### Monitoring

AI can help summarize performance movement, surface anomalies, and prepare first-pass risk notes.

### Reporting

AI can turn raw channel updates into leadership-ready summaries, provided the underlying data is trusted.

### QA

AI can check campaign names, briefs, landing pages, stakeholder updates, and readouts against standards.

### Knowledge management

AI can turn repeated work into reusable playbooks, templates, and decision logic.

### Drafting

AI can create first drafts of briefs, memos, email updates, and executive summaries. Human judgment still owns tone, tradeoffs, and final decisions.

## Where AI is risky

AI should not be trusted blindly for:

- Legal-sensitive language
- Confidential claims
- Unsupported metrics
- Final executive judgment
- Data interpretation when source quality is unclear
- Stakeholder-sensitive communication without review

## Adoption model

### 1. Start with real workflows

Do not start with tools. Start with repetitive work that already exists.

### 2. Build narrow helpers

The best early AI workflows are focused:

- Summarize this readout
- Check this memo for political risk
- Turn this audit into a decision brief
- Convert this roadmap into a template
- Extract action items from this meeting note

### 3. Preserve human judgment

AI should compress work, not launder accountability.

### 4. Encode standards

Every repeated workflow should create a reusable instruction, checklist, or template.

### 5. Measure adoption

Useful AI is used. If people avoid the workflow, the design is wrong.

## Standard

AI should make the operating system lighter, not more theatrical.
