# Executive Reporting Model

## Principle

Executive reporting is not a data dump. It is decision support.

A good readout should help leaders understand what changed, why it matters, what risk exists, and what decision is needed.

## The structure

### 1. Bottom line

Start with the answer.

Example:

`Volume is slightly behind forecast, but the larger risk is signal quality. We should hold spend until the missing source data is resolved, then shift budget toward the higher-quality path.`

### 2. What changed

State the material movement:

- Volume
- Cost
- Quality
- Funnel progression
- Channel mix
- Pacing
- Forecast variance

### 3. Why it happened

Separate facts from interpretation.

Use:

- Known drivers
- Likely drivers
- Unknowns
- Data limitations

### 4. What it means

Translate performance into business implication.

- Is this a short-term pacing issue?
- Is this a structural conversion issue?
- Is this a channel mix problem?
- Is this a measurement problem?
- Does leadership need to decide something?

### 5. Recommended action

Give a clear next move.

Options:

- Hold
- Shift
- Pause
- Scale
- Test
- Escalate
- Investigate
- Reforecast

## Readout template

```md
## Bottom line

[One to three sentences. Lead with the business implication.]

## What changed

- [Metric or signal]
- [Metric or signal]
- [Metric or signal]

## Why it matters

[Explain the business implication, not just the channel movement.]

## What we recommend

[Clear action and owner.]

## Watchouts

[Known risks, data caveats, or dependencies.]
```

## Standard

If leadership cannot tell what decision is needed, the report is not finished.
