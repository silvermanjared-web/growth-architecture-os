# Agency Governance Model

## Principle

Agencies should be accountable to standards, not vibes.

A strong agency model defines who owns strategy, who owns execution, how work is reviewed, how risk escalates, and how decisions are made.

## Operating roles

### Internal team

Owns:

- Business objective
- Budget logic
- Strategic direction
- Measurement standards
- Final decisions
- Stakeholder communication

### Agency or partner

Owns:

- Execution quality
- Platform management
- Testing recommendations
- Documentation
- QA participation
- Timely escalation of risk

### Analytics

Owns:

- Source-of-truth reporting
- Funnel definitions
- Dashboard logic
- Measurement QA
- Readout support

## Cadence

### Weekly

- Spend and pacing
- Performance movement
- Risk and anomalies
- Active tests
- Required decisions

### Biweekly

- Creative and landing-path review
- Search query or audience insight review
- Funnel-quality review
- Test readout

### Monthly

- Budget allocation review
- Scope and workload review
- Strategic roadmap refresh
- Executive summary

### Quarterly

- Agency scorecard
- SOW fit
- Capability gaps
- In-housing opportunities
- Contract or staffing adjustments

## Agency scorecard

Score agencies on:

- Performance against goal
- Quality of strategic thinking
- Execution discipline
- QA reliability
- Responsiveness
- Documentation quality
- Proactive risk surfacing
- Ability to simplify complexity for leadership

## Escalation standard

Escalate based on business impact, timing, and reversibility.

Do not escalate every annoyance. Do not bury material risk.

## Standard

The best agency relationships are clear, fair, and direct. No ambushes. No vague frustration. No outsourcing of strategy.
