# Performance Media Operating Model

## Purpose

Performance media should not run as a collection of disconnected channel tactics.

A strong operating model connects budget, signal quality, funnel movement, creative, agency execution, and leadership decisions into one manageable system.

## The model

### 1. Business objective

Start with the business outcome, not the platform.

Examples:

- Enrolled student
- Qualified lead
- Approved application
- Revenue
- Retention
- Pipeline quality

The media system should optimize toward the outcome that actually matters, while using intermediate metrics only as diagnostic signals.

### 2. Demand reality

Clarify where demand actually lives.

Questions:

- Is the market demand-capture heavy or demand-generation heavy?
- Is seasonality driving volume or quality changes?
- Are constraints operational, geographic, compliance-related, or budget-related?
- Which audiences are highest intent?
- Which audiences require more education before conversion?

### 3. Channel role

Every channel needs a job.

- Search: intent capture, competitor pressure, local or category demand
- Paid social: audience creation, proof, retargeting, lead generation
- YouTube / video: consideration, reassurance, objection handling
- PMAX: scaled conversion capture when signal quality is strong enough
- Programmatic / CTV: reach, education, sequential messaging, market presence
- Retargeting: convert non-converters with clearer proof and lower-friction next steps

### 4. Measurement integrity

Before optimizing, confirm the system can be trusted.

Check:

- Conversion definitions
- Duplicate events
- Missing campaign codes
- Call versus form attribution
- CRM handoff quality
- Lead status definitions
- Offline conversion uploads
- Enhanced conversion coverage
- Platform and source-of-truth variance

### 5. Allocation logic

Budget should move by decision rules, not mood.

Define:

- When to shift budget
- When to hold budget
- When to pause a tactic
- When to add upper-funnel support
- When signal quality is too weak for automation
- When volume is cheap but business quality is poor

### 6. Creative and landing path

Media does not convert in isolation.

Message, proof, offer, landing path, and follow-up all affect performance. A media plan without landing-path accountability is incomplete.

### 7. Operating cadence

The system needs a rhythm:

- Weekly pacing and risk read
- Channel-level diagnostic review
- Agency / partner QA
- Funnel-quality review
- Executive decision summary
- Monthly allocation review
- Quarterly operating-model reset

## Decision output

A good performance media operating model should produce clear answers:

- What is working?
- What is distorted?
- What cannot be trusted yet?
- Where should spend move?
- What is the risk if we do nothing?
- Who owns the next step?

## Standard

The goal is not more reporting. The goal is fewer arguments about what the reporting means.
