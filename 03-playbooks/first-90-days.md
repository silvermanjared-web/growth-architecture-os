# First 90 Days Playbook

## Core idea

Stabilize the system. Then scale it.

The first 90 days should create control: clearer ownership, cleaner signals, faster decisions, and less dependency on heroics.

## The point

Growth rarely breaks because the team lacks tactics.

It breaks because the system is unclear.

The work is not to show up with a bag of tricks. The work is to map the machine, identify where decisions get distorted, and build enough operating discipline that good choices become easier to repeat.

## What gets diagnosed

### 1. Demand reality

Where volume, quality, seasonality, and constraints actually live.

### 2. Measurement integrity

What is tracked, trusted, missing, duplicated, or under-attributed.

### 3. Media allocation

How budget moves across search, social, video, PMAX, calls, and forms.

### 4. Agency and team model

Who owns decisions, who executes, and where accountability breaks.

### 5. Leadership cadence

How risk reaches the right people before it becomes a surprise.

## Days 1-30: Listen hard. Map reality. Find the leaks.

Do not start by prescribing. Start by building a shared view of what is actually happening, where the risk sits, and which fixes will matter soonest.

### Work

- Audit media mix, measurement stack, agencies, team model, and funnel flow
- Align leadership goals, stakeholder expectations, metric definitions, and decision rights
- Separate urgent risk, quick wins, and deeper rebuilds
- Create a simple readout: what works, what is risky, what needs a call

### Output

- Diagnostic memo
- Risk map
- Metric dictionary
- Agency readout
- Sequenced plan

## Days 31-60: Rebuild the layer that makes execution trustworthy.

This is where the diagnostic becomes operating infrastructure.

Not a giant transformation program. A focused rebuild of the parts that distort spend, reporting, accountability, and speed.

### Work

- Tighten conversion definitions and source-of-truth reporting
- Clarify call and form visibility
- Define budget logic across channels
- Build a CRO and creative test queue
- Install agency standards and QA checkpoints
- Create leadership-ready decision outputs

### Output

- Measurement plan
- Allocation rules
- CRO queue
- Agency scorecard
- Weekly risk read

## Days 61-90: Move from recovery mode to operating rhythm.

By this point, the question should shift from `what is broken?` to `how do we run this without constant escalation?`

### Work

- Establish weekly executive read
- Create agency operating rhythm
- Train internal owners on standards and workflows
- Monitor pacing, signal quality, funnel movement, and channel mix
- Make next tests and budget moves clear before the room leaves

### Output

- Repeatable cadence
- Owner map
- Decision rules
- Exception-based management rhythm
- Next-quarter roadmap

## Political QA

No takeover theater. No surprise blame. No vague ownership.

The people around the system usually know more than the artifacts show. The work is to make the truth usable without making the room defensive.

### Rules

- Protect owner credibility
- Separate process issues from personal judgment
- Clarify who recommends, approves, executes, and escalates
- Make agencies accountable through standards and cadence
- Escalate by risk, timing, and reversibility

## What leadership should have by day 90

- Clear truth: what is working, what is distorted, and what cannot be trusted yet
- Decision logic: when to shift spend, escalate risk, pause work, or push harder
- Owner clarity: who owns the decision, execution, QA, and follow-up
- Repeatable rhythm: a cadence that keeps pressure moving without manufacturing urgency

## Standard

By day 90, leadership should know:

- What is working
- What is risky
- Who owns what
- Where to invest next
