# CRO Roadmap Playbook

## Core idea

Do not start with tests. Start with the conversion system.

A strong CRO roadmap explains where intent exists, where friction blocks action, where proof is missing, what measurement is required, who owns the work, and how the sequence creates learning.

## Roadmap structure

### 1. Situation

What is the current system doing?

Questions:

- Which pages or paths receive the highest-intent traffic?
- Which paths are paid, organic, direct, referral, or returning?
- Where do users encounter friction?
- Are different paths asking for different levels of commitment?
- Are the strongest proof points visible near the decision?

### 2. Diagnosis

What is actually broken?

Common findings:

- High-intent visitors get high-friction paths
- Proof is buried in PDFs, FAQs, or deep pages
- CTAs compete with each other
- Forms split data into inconsistent structures
- Tracking cannot see the full funnel
- Portal or CRM steps are invisible to marketing

### 3. Thesis

What principle should guide the roadmap?

Example:

`Optimize enrollments, not form fills.`

A cheaper lead that never reaches the business outcome is not cheaper.

### 4. Prioritized queue

Each test or workstream should include:

- ID
- Name
- Location
- Impact
- Effort
- Phase
- Dependency
- Primary metric
- Guardrail

### 5. Phasing

Sequence by dependency:

- Phase 0: instrumentation and taxonomy
- Phase 1: credibility and reassurance
- Phase 2: structural friction reduction
- Phase 3: nurture, retargeting, and scale

### 6. Ownership

Use a RACI so the roadmap does not become a suggestion list.

Roles often include:

- Executive sponsor
- Performance media
- CRO agency
- Experimentation operations
- Content
- Creative
- Development
- Analytics

### 7. Scorecard

Use the business outcome as the primary metric.

Supporting metrics may include:

- Form start
- Form completion
- Connect rate
- Application start
- Application submit
- Enrollment or sale
- Drop-off by step
- Quality rate

## Monday actions

Every roadmap should end with what can ship this week.

Examples:

- Remove stale modal
- Fix confusing CTA
- Add proof near form
- Stand up missing tracking
- Agree unified taxonomy
- Walk the full enrollment or sales path

## Standard

The roadmap should make the next decision obvious.
