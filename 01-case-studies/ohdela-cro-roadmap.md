# Case Study: Enrollment Conversion Roadmap

## Context

A multi-site enrollment funnel had two different front doors serving different traffic types.

The paid funnel was modern, direct, and built around a simple lead path. The organic site carried more depth, SEO value, proof, and high-intent visitor behavior, but routed users into a higher-friction enrollment experience.

The core insight was simple: **the warmest traffic was getting the hardest path.**

## Problem

The system looked like a traffic challenge, but the stronger diagnosis was a conversion challenge.

High-intent visitors were arriving with more context and stronger implied intent, but they encountered:

- Competing calls to action
- A higher-friction account-first enrollment path
- Buried credibility signals
- Inconsistent form logic
- Underdeveloped portal instrumentation
- Proof points that existed but were not positioned near conversion moments

The issue was not that the organization lacked assets. The issue was that the assets were disconnected from the decision path.

## Diagnosis

The roadmap separated the problem into five practical layers:

1. **Friction:** where the experience added effort before value was clear
2. **Proof:** where credibility existed but was not visible at the moment of doubt
3. **Taxonomy:** where inconsistent form and funnel signals weakened optimization
4. **Instrumentation:** where the team could not see enough of the funnel to manage it well
5. **Ownership:** where work needed a clear RACI to move without ambiguity

## Strategic thesis

Optimize enrollments, not form fills.

A cheaper lead that never enrolls is a more expensive school.

The roadmap was built around four principles:

- De-friction the warm path first
- Borrow earned proof instead of inventing new persuasion
- Create one taxonomy and one bidding signal
- Measure before moving structural pieces

## Operating move

The work was sequenced into phases instead of treated as a wish list.

### Phase 0: Foundation

- Unify grade bands, fields, and conversion events
- Instrument funnel and portal behavior
- Remove stale friction points
- Establish baseline performance and site-health measures

### Phase 1: Credibility and reassurance

- Surface accreditation, access support, and proof points near conversion moments
- Add parent reassurance messaging
- Clarify the role of certified teachers versus parent responsibilities
- Add advisor and testimonial trust cues

### Phase 2: Structural conversion work

- Rebuild the form path into a lower-friction experience
- Move toward application before account creation where feasible
- Collapse competing CTAs into clearer intent paths
- Improve enhanced conversion and server-side signal quality

### Phase 3: Capture and scale

- Add retargeting and nurture for non-converters
- Refresh design and message match
- Roll proven patterns into adjacent state-brand properties

## Scorecard

The roadmap used cost per enrolled student as the primary business metric, supported by funnel metrics:

- Form start
- Form completion
- Advisor connect
- Application
- Enrollment
- Portal account create to submit drop-off

Guardrails included lead quality, site performance, and organic visibility.

## Why this matters

This case study shows the difference between CRO as button testing and CRO as operating-model design.

The work was not just about changing page elements. It was about making the enrollment engine more measurable, more credible, easier to act on, and easier to scale.

## Reusable lesson

The best CRO roadmaps do not start with test ideas. They start with the system:

- Where is intent highest?
- Where is friction highest?
- Where is proof missing?
- What can we measure?
- Who owns the next move?

Fix that, and testing becomes a disciplined operating rhythm instead of a pile of experiments.
