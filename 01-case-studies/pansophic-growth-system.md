# Case Study: Multi-Brand Education Growth System

## Context

A PE-backed education environment with a large multi-brand portfolio needed performance media, CRO, measurement, agency work, and in-housing decisions to operate as one system.

The portfolio included online schools, brick-and-mortar schools, early learning academies, university partnerships, and international hybrid school models.

The growth challenge was not just channel performance. It was system performance.

## Problem

The environment had many moving parts:

- Multiple business units
- Multiple agencies and vendors
- Paid search, paid social, PMAX, programmatic, YouTube, CTV, and brand media
- Enrollment operations dependencies
- CRO and web development dependencies
- Seasonal demand cycles
- Executive visibility needs

In that kind of environment, performance can degrade even when individual teams are working hard. The risk is fragmentation: each team optimizes its own piece while the portfolio lacks a shared view of spend, pacing, signal quality, funnel movement, and ownership.

## Diagnosis

The operating model needed stronger connective tissue across five layers:

1. Media allocation logic
2. Measurement and conversion standards
3. Agency accountability
4. CRO and landing-path discipline
5. Executive decision cadence

The practical goal was to reduce noise, protect active demand, and make tradeoffs easier to see.

## Operating moves

### Reframe performance as a portfolio system

Instead of treating each school, channel, or agency as an isolated surface, the work focused on shared logic: spend, pacing, funnel quality, source-of-truth reporting, and escalation standards.

### Rebuild CRO as an operating model

CRO was organized into a practical 5-workstream model:

- Foundation work
- Test queue
- State rollout
- Measurement cadence
- Operating model

This made experimentation less ad hoc and more manageable across agency, web, analytics, and business stakeholders.

### Correct channel mix where the funnel showed distortion

In one brick-and-mortar school environment, media had become too search-heavy. Reintegrating YouTube into the channel mix helped improve the downstream enrollment signal and informed future allocation planning.

### Protect in-housing from becoming disruption

Performance marketing in-housing can create risk if it is treated only as cost reduction. The work required protecting active peak-season spend, clarifying which capabilities should move internally, and keeping agencies accountable during transition.

## Resulting operating model

The system moved toward clearer standards across:

- Budget allocation
- Campaign structure
- Conversion definitions
- CRO test governance
- Agency readouts
- Executive escalation
- Measurement QA

## Why this matters

The value was not just better media management. The value was better business control.

Leadership needs to know where demand is healthy, where signal is distorted, where spend should move, and what risks require a decision. That requires an operating layer, not just campaign commentary.

## Reusable lesson

Complex growth portfolios need an operating model before they need more tactics.

The operating model should answer:

- What do we trust?
- What do we not trust yet?
- Who owns each decision?
- When does risk escalate?
- What is the next move?
