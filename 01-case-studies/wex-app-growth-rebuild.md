# Case Study: App Growth Rebuild

## Context

A financial services app needed stronger acquisition performance and a cleaner operating model across paid search, quality assurance, and measurement.

The program was not failing because the market lacked demand. It was failing because the acquisition system did not make demand easy enough to identify, qualify, optimize, or govern.

## Challenge

The paid search program had the symptoms of an account that had outgrown its original operating structure. Campaign architecture, KPI alignment, QA routines, pacing discipline, and app-led measurement were not working together cleanly enough for the growth stage.

That created a common performance trap: media activity was visible, but the operating signal was not strong enough. The team needed clearer separation between volume, quality, and approved-application outcomes so optimization could move toward the right business signal instead of chasing surface-level efficiency.

## Approach

Led a full-funnel rebuild across acquisition structure, measurement logic, and operating discipline.

Key moves included:

- Reworked paid search account structure around clearer intent, funnel stage, and optimization purpose
- Tightened KPI alignment so campaign performance could be judged against application-stage outcomes, not only traffic or lead activity
- Added stronger QA routines to catch tracking, pacing, budget, and execution issues earlier
- Improved pacing discipline so spend decisions were easier to inspect and adjust
- Systematized app-led measurement so the program could distinguish stronger demand from noisier demand
- Aligned media, analytics, product, and creative inputs so the acquisition system had a clearer feedback loop

The mechanism mattered. Better structure improved signal quality. Better signal quality gave the platforms and operators cleaner optimization inputs. Stronger QA reduced waste and kept execution errors from distorting performance reads. The rebuild did not depend on one bidding trick; it made the acquisition system easier to learn from and easier to manage.

## Outcome

Approved applications grew ~500% after the rebuild.

This is an application-stage metric, not a revenue, margin, or enterprise-value claim. The point of the result is narrower and stronger: when account structure, KPI alignment, QA, and measurement are rebuilt around the right operating signal, paid acquisition can move from activity management to performance architecture.

## Jared's role

Owned the operating diagnosis and rebuild logic across media structure, measurement alignment, QA discipline, and cross-functional coordination. The work required more than campaign optimization. It required turning a fragmented acquisition motion into a cleaner growth system with better signal, clearer controls, and a result leadership could understand.

## Reusable lesson

Performance improves when the account rebuild is paired with operating discipline. Structure, signal quality, and QA matter as much as bidding tactics.

The practical lesson is simple: do not scale spend against a weak signal. Fix the operating system first. Once the signal is cleaner, optimization becomes more trustworthy and performance conversations become more useful.
