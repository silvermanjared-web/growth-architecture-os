# Case Study: Paid Media Division Reset

## Context

A large paid media division was scaling quickly and needed stronger operating discipline to preserve quality, margin, client confidence, and delivery consistency.

The work involved enterprise client strategy and delivery systems across $10M+ in annual client media.

## Problem

Rapid growth can expose the difference between talent and operating model.

When a division grows quickly, the symptoms are familiar:

- Inconsistent QA
- Variable client delivery quality
- Too much dependency on individual operators
- Weak handoff standards
- Margin pressure from rework
- Strategy and execution drifting apart
- Leaders seeing issues after they have already become client problems

The fix was not just to ask people to work harder. The fix was to redesign the system around clearer routines and standards.

## Diagnosis

The operating model needed clearer answers to basic questions:

- What does good look like?
- Who reviews the work?
- Where does strategic accountability sit?
- Which routines protect quality before the client sees the issue?
- Which client opportunities require a new POV or capability roadmap?

## Operating moves

### Rebuilt delivery discipline

Created clearer QA systems and performance routines so the division could scale work without simply adding more heroics.

### Improved investment accountability

Helped enterprise client work connect more directly to performance logic, budget discipline, and full-funnel outcomes.

### Built executive-facing POVs

Developed capability audits and channel roadmaps that opened new growth paths across YouTube, PMAX, and LinkedIn.

### Delivered measurable client impact

For WEX, a phased account rebuild and tighter investment discipline drove a 500% lift in approved applications.

## Why this matters

The work showed that agency growth depends on operating discipline, not just account talent.

Strong paid media teams need systems for quality, escalation, learning, and strategic consistency. Without those systems, growth creates complexity faster than teams can absorb it.

## Reusable lesson

Scaling a paid media division requires three forms of infrastructure:

1. Delivery infrastructure: QA, process, handoffs, standards
2. Strategic infrastructure: POVs, audits, roadmap logic
3. Commercial infrastructure: scope, margin, client accountability

When those layers align, the division can grow without becoming fragile.
